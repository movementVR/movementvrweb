Gain change smoothing When there is a sharp change in gain from one trial to the next, (e.g., baseline to abrupt adaptation, or adaptation to washout), gain change smoothing can avoid sharp jumps in hand position. By default, gain change is smoothed as follows:    1) The gain change begins when hands are in their home position      (because is close to the origin, the perturbation effect is close to zero, reducing potential jumps in hand position)    2) The gain is changed smoothly over 0.1 seconds.
